The History of Synthesizers

  • Pre-1960s
  • 1960s
  • 1970s
  • 1980s
  • 1990s
  • 2000s
  • Today

Pre-1960s

Some predecessors to synthesizers were released in the early 20th century. Some examples are the Hammond organ and the theremin, which you may have heard in old films such as Alfred Hitchcock's horror classics. What is widely regarded as the first synthesizer, the RCA Mark II, is released in the early 1950s.

1960s

The Moog Modular, one of the most famous synthesizers of all time, hit the scene in the mid 60s. Don Buchla's synthesizer actually predates the Moog by a few years.

1970s

Smaller Moog and Arp synthesizers were released. Synthesizers began to become commonplace in several musical genres such as progressive rock, disco, funk, jazz, and krautrock.

1980s

Analog synthesizers are gradually replaced by digital ones. MIDI technology is invented. Roland, Yamaha, and Korg products become popular. New wave, industrial, Detroit techno, and hip hop feature many synthesized sounds.

1990s

Computer software that can be controlled by MIDI interfaces is invented. Electronic music is popular, e.g. techno, electronica, house, jungle, and trance. A lot of this music relied heavily on samplers instead of synthesizers, but the Korg M1 was still popular in this era.

2000s

Synthesizers start to get small and more affordable. Synthesizers like the microKorg and the Alesis Micron are a fraction of the size of earlier synths. Korg even makes a synthesizer program for the Nintendo DS.

Today

Synthesizers continue to shrink. You could fit every model in the Korg Volca series on one desk. Some, like Teenage Engineering drum machines, can fit in a pocket. Music software is available on smartphones and tablets.
