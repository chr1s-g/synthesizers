What is a synthesizer?

It may look like a piano or organ with knobs, but what is happening internally is very different compared to an acoustic instrument. Inside of a synthesizer, there are oscillators. These oscillators make the sound.

The simplest waveform that can be generated is a sine wave. There are also triangle, square, and sawtooth waveforms. Multiple waves can be played at the same time.

A filter can then be applied to remove or emphasize certain frequencies. For example, a hi-pass filter only allows frequencies above a certain point to pass through.

The volume as a function of time can also be manipulated using the knobs labeled attack, decay, sustain, and release.
